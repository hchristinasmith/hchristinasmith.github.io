What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is the ability to recognise, understand, manage, and influence emotions â€” both my own and those of others. It includes being self-aware, practicing self-regulation, staying motivated, showing empathy, and having strong social skills.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Aspect EQ (Emotional Quotient) IQ (Intelligence Quotient)
Focus Emotions, relationships, empathy Logical reasoning, problem-solving, knowledge
Skills Self-awareness, empathy, communication Math, language, spatial, memory skills
Measurement Harder to quantify; often behavior-based Standardized tests and scores
Application Managing people, handling stress/conflict Solving technical or academic problems

Why is EQ important?

  • Connect with others, build trust, and resolve conflicts smoothly
  • Inspire, support, and bring out the best in others
  • Be in tune with your emotions
  • Reduce stress and prevent burnout
  • Bounce back from setbacks with perspective and control
  • Build teams that communicate well and perform more cohesively
